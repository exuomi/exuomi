              Date: 2025
              Theme: Speculative Design
    
            Project Jøkulkyrkja to Preserve Population.
        
    
            ABSTRACT. With the collapse of the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation, 
            warm current that regulates temperature also collapses. Coupled with a new grand solar 
            minimum, the temperature drops drastically and gets colder each year. Sea level rises 
            and extreme snow piling covers much of the existing infrastructure. Outdoor conditions 
            have become unstable and unsafe for those without specific PPE. 



            Project Jøkulkyrkja proposes self-sustaining stations, that house 20-35 people, spread
            relatively even thorughout the city above snow. 



            Shepherds would be in charge of the station and keeping occupants safe, along with AI 
            supercomputer Prime Messenger that forecasts the upcoming weather conditions along with
            IoT and operational resources. 






            This speculative project proposes a widespread government response to a severe natural 
            disaster, and explores gradual procedures that develop into the bunker presented. The 
            features aim to address the anticipated problems that arise with extreme weather 
            conditions, including ensuring quality of life for occpuants. 


            The bunker's form builds on top of the existing shape of buildings present in the 
            courtyard, prioritising functionality over stylistic choices, taking inspiration 
            from RAE's Antarctic Vostok Station. Any components that protrude out generally
            slope at an angle to avoid excessive snowfall accumulation.

              Date: 2025
              Theme: Public Art
    
            Chrono:Hall to see the heart of Ottawa in new dimensions.
        
    
            ABSTRACT. Appearing with more similarities to a booth despite the name, 
            Chrono:Hall aims to depict larger spaces from within, through the use of screens
            and other immersive effects. Located in the heart of Ottawa, those passing by 
            could enter and select whether to see visions of the past or future, with 
            additional scenarios according to their decision. The significance of such an 
            installation is to allow any interested party to learn more about Ottawa's 
            history, or see speculative visions for the future of the city. Every vision 
            is linked to the location of the Chrono:Hall that the viewer is visiting, but 
            allows for different viewpoints as well. This allows for the possibility of 
            future installations at other areas of interest and historical significance. 



            The creation of each scenario involves a group that collaborates to produce the 
            concept, design, 3D imagery, sound, and coding involved to display on the screens.
            Limited spots would be available for commissioning members that form the groups 
            to work on a balanced and varied amount of projects. 


            

            Inside, the controls should reflect the wide audience variety and be as intuitive
            as possible for anyone to understand and operate with ease. A haptic knob that 
            doubles as a button placed in a way that mirrors a car dashboard creates an 
            environment familiar to most. Furthermore, a small info dashboard beside the knob 
            avoids clutter on the big screen while still displaying important information. 




            The booth's form appears mechanical and futuristic, appearing as a sort of time 
            machine. The external material would be coated in acrylic mirrors to reflect the 
            surroundings due to the location-based projects, and the designs allow for the 
            thickness needed to support the electronics that allow the project to function. 
  



            The result is an installation that brings foot traffic to essential locations in 
            Ottawa, while educating viewers of significant history as well as picture future 
            developments, in an easy and entertaining experience for both tourists and locals.
